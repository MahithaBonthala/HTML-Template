1. Privacy policy page: If your website collects personal information from visitors, such as email addresses or payment information, it's important to have a privacy policy page that explains how this information is collected, used, and protected.

2. Terms and conditions page: A terms and conditions page outlines the rules and regulations that govern the use of your website. It can cover topics such as copyright, user conduct, and disclaimers.

3. Careers page: If your business is hiring, consider creating a careers page where visitors can learn about job opportunities and apply for open positions.

4. Press kit page: If you want to make it easy for journalists and media outlets to cover your business, consider creating a press kit page that includes high-resolution images, company information, and other resources.

5. Investor relations page: If your business is publicly traded or seeking investment, consider creating an investor relations page that includes financial reports, investor information, and other relevant resources.

6. Community page: A community page can be a great way to build engagement and foster a sense of community among your visitors. Consider including a forum, blog, or other interactive features on this page.

7. Support page: If your business offers customer support, consider creating a support page where visitors can find answers to common questions, submit support requests, or access other resources.
